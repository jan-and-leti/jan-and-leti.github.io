Travel

The best way to get around Galicia is to rent a car at one of the airports or using local taxis.

Flying In

If you are flying in, you have different airports to choose from depending where you are coming from:

  1. Vigo (VGO) - 20 mins to Baiona
  2. Santiago de Compostela (SCQ) - 75 mins to Baiona
  3. Oporto(OPO) - 90 mins to Baiona

From the airport you can take a taxi or there are buses available (although check timetables). You can also hire a car at the airport which will give you flexibility to move around and explore the area.

Train

If you are travelling from Madrid, or elsewhere in Spain, you can arrive to the centre of Vigo with the high-speed train.

Car Rental

There are several car rental options, and the taxi services are relatively inexpensive.

Recommendations

Activities

Galicia has its own language, cuisine, culture (bagpipes!), and architecture. We recommend that you take advantage of what it has to offer. The list below is a good start.



  • Ancient Pilgrimate City

    Santiago de Compostela
    • Santiago de Compostela

    Santiago de Compostela, the capital of Galicia, Spain, is famous for being the culmination of the Camino de Santiago pilgrimage route, with the grand Santiago de Compostela Cathedral as its centerpiece. The city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, celebrated for its well-preserved medieval architecture and vibrant cultural life. Visitors can explore the historic old town, with its narrow streets, charming squares, and lively markets. The city also boasts numerous museums, such as the Museum of the Galician People and the Pilgrimage Museum. Additionally, the lively atmosphere of its tapas bars and restaurants makes it a delightful culinary destination.

    Santiago de Compostela has been one of the 3 main pilgrimage centres in Europe for centuries (the other two are Rome and Jerusalem). The cathedral has a very rich story. The remains of St James are buried there. Check mass times and allow plenty of time if you want to attend, long queues are formed outside. Visits during masses are not permitted. The Pilgrims' mass is famous for the Botafumeiro, a massive censer that is flown across the transept pulled by 8 skilful men.

    For the adventurous people, we recommend doing one day of the Camino (around 20-30km walk). There are two options: the Portuguese way and the Spanish way.

    For more information, visit https://www.spain.info/en/destination/santiago-compostela/

  • Bustling Coastal Town

    Baiona
    • Baiona

    Baiona is a bustling coastal town with a rich history and stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean. The town is known for its charming old town, beautiful beaches, and lively atmosphere. It was the focus for trade in the region until the rise of Vigo that took place at the end of the 19th century.

    The town's most attractive streets are set around the bay, which opens up next to the mouth of Miñor River. It was here that La Pinta moored in 1493, the first caravel to reach port after the voyage of discovery to the New World. One can see the replica of the boat. The arrival of La Pinta is at the heart of a celebration held every year on 1 March.

    Don't miss the sunset from Monterreal Fort and its famous Torre del Príncipe (Prince's Tower). It has three kilometres of defensive walls and is now a Parador Hotel. If you are in the area, and are up for some challenge, you can go to the Virgin of the Rock (Virgen de la Roca), pay some euros and climb to her hand for great views. Or drive a bit further up to La Grova for breathtaking scenery. We recommend the use of GPS and paper maps.

    For more information, visit https://www.spain.info/en/destination/baiona/.

  • One-day Excursion

    Cíes Islands
    • Cíes Islands

    The Cíes Islands are the Vigo estuary's greatest treasure: an amazing national marine-terrestrial park and one of the country's most beautiful places, which is why the Romans named them the islands of the gods.

    The views from the lighthouse at Cies Islands allow you to perceive the curve of the horizon line. The hike up takes around 45 minutes.

    Boats to the islands depart from Baiona and Vigo.

    Imporant: If you would like to visit the islands, you need to book ahead. It is a natural park and to protect the fauna and flora, the number of daily visitors is limited. Before you book a boat with the shipping company, you need to obtain permission to visit the islands from the local authority.

  • A Spanish historic city on the border with Portugal

    Tui
    • Tui, Spain

    Tui, Spain is renowned for its stunning historical architecture, most notably the Tui Cathedral, a mix of Romanesque and Gothic styles. The city is located on the banks of the Miño River, providing picturesque views and opportunities for riverside walks. Visitors can explore the old town with its narrow medieval streets and well-preserved buildings. Tui is also famous for the International Bridge, which connects Spain to Portugal, offering a unique cross-border experience. Additionally, the nearby Monte Aloia Natural Park is a great spot for hiking and enjoying nature.

    For more information, visit https://www.spain.info/en/destination/tui/

  • Beach

    Playa America
    • Playa America

    This is one of the most popular beaches in Galicia. It has all kinds of infrastructure and shares an extensive sandy beach with Panxón beach. From it you can see all of Baiona as well as the Las Estelas Islands.

    The beach is great for walks on the beach, with small wooden walkways to cross the small channels that form when the tide is high. It is great for playing sports with low tide and you can also rent a boat for water skiing and jet skis.

    For more information, visit https://www.spain.info/en/beach/america/

  • Beach

    Playa Patos
    • Playa Patos

    One of the biggest beaches in the Vigo estuary, with more than three kilometres of sandy ground. There are views of the Islas Cíes (Cíes Islands) opposite. It is set in just the right position to enjoy beautiful sunsets. Patos is great for surf lovers and it has many places to enjoy a drink and good music.

    For more information, visit https://www.spain.info/en/beach/patos-nigran/



Other Cities

The capital of the Pontevedra province is the city of Pontevedra, with nice streets and a church. Vigo is very important as well, industrial city that thrived in shipbuilding and the conserveras. The Citroen factory gives jobs to a great part of the population.

Recommendations

Restaurants and Bars

Galicia is famous for its food and it is the home of Albariño and Ribeiro white wines. We encourage you to explore!

We have listed a few places that Leti's family have enjoyed (for decades!). Additionally, using Google Maps / Foursquare and other apps will have a lot more recommendations.



Baiona

Mercadillo de Sabaris

On Mondays mornings, early afternoons, there is el mercadillo de Sabaris (flea market): just stroll along the different stalls. We love to buy empanadas there, amazing loaves of bread (try the pan de maiz), the famous Galician cheese (Queso de Tetilla) and quince (stall in front of the church, a very big one), coffee liqueur. Stop for a coffee and a pastry at Panaderia Yajoma, Julian Valverde 69, 36393 at Galician prices. All the products are locally sourced. There are plenty of other things like clothes, swimsuits, shoes, etc.

Meson Fidalgo

The Meson Fidalgo, Rua Julian Valverde 79, 36393, is a great option for tapas or dinner.

Main Street of Baiona - bars, cafés, bakeries, ice cream

In Baiona you should try the small tapas bars populating the old part of the village, those small, cobblestoned streets peppering Baiona. On the main street, Avenida Monterreal, you will find all sorts of tempting things like, waffles, ice creams, pastries, burgers, pizzas. And the iconic Churreria Emilin for hot chocolate and churros: a must try.



Nigrán

Taperia O'Monte

Taperia O'Monte, unknown to tourists, is great for trying different types of tortillas and other Galician specialities. Situated on top of a hill, you will have to follow the signposts or use GPS. Great for families, better booking in advance: +34 609804666. They also provide accommodation.

Restaurante Asador Los Abetos

Restaurante Asador Los Abetos, good for beefeaters, open fire grill.

Casa Alberto

A restaurant with stunning views of the Cíes Islands, especially if you get the seat on the terrace. Casa Alberto can be found at: Rua do Torreiro, 1, 36391 Priegue.



Santiago de Compostella

If you are flying to/from Santiago, there is a very good restaurant 1 minute from the airport, Casa Lorenzo, Lavacolla, s/n, 15820 Santiago de Compostela, La Coruña. We like also A Casa da Vina, Rua de San Lazaro, 54, 15703 Santiago de Compostela (closed on Mondays), away from the places near the cathedral, that are overpriced and busy.



Playa America and Playa Patos

Playa America

Get a coffee/food and enjoy the views from La Genuina café. The northern end of the beach has very good places for tapas and food, like El Pescador, Punto e Coma, or JR.



Playa Patos

We love Maui Beach Patos and La Ola (outdoor bar). Also in the Patos area, there are good places to eat: La Piccola Italia pizzeria, Taperia O'Druida, pop up food trucks or great ice creams at Xuntos (there is another one in Playa America).

What is the weather like in Baiona in June?
The weather in Baiona in June is usually warm and sunny. The average temperature is around 20°C (68°F) with highs of 24°C (75°F) and lows of 16°C (61°F). The weather is usually dry with an average of 8 days of rain in the month.
